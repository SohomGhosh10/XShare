Experience Overview

Amazing experience with Google's interview process. The technical rounds were challenging but fair, and the interviewers were very supportive throughout the process. The entire journey taught me a lot about problem-solving and system design thinking.

The process took about 3 weeks from the initial application to the final decision. Google's recruiting team was very professional and kept me updated at every step. Even though the interviews were tough, I felt they were designed to bring out the best in candidates rather than to trip them up.

Tags

DSA System Design Coding Full-time On-campus

Interview Rounds

1

Technical Round

45 minutes

Questions Asked:

  • Coding Problem: Given a binary tree, find the maximum path sum. The path can start and end at any node in the tree.
  • Follow-up: What if we need to track the actual path as well?
  • System Design: Design a URL shortener like bit.ly (high-level design only)

My Approach:

For the binary tree problem, I used a recursive approach with DFS. I maintained a global variable to track the maximum sum and for each node, calculated the maximum path sum that could be achieved by including that node. The interviewer appreciated my clear explanation of the time complexity (O(n)) and space complexity (O(h) where h is height).

For the system design question, I started with clarifying requirements, then discussed the basic components: URL encoding/decoding, database schema, caching strategy, and load balancing.

2

Technical Round

45 minutes

Questions Asked:

  • Coding Problem: Implement LRU Cache with O(1) get and put operations
  • Optimization: Discuss different approaches and trade-offs
  • Computer Science Fundamentals: Questions about databases, indexing, and query optimization

My Approach:

I implemented LRU Cache using a combination of HashMap and Doubly Linked List. Explained how HashMap provides O(1) lookup while the linked list maintains the order of usage. The interviewer tested edge cases and I handled them well.

For the CS fundamentals, we discussed B+ trees, database indexing strategies, and query execution plans. This round felt more like a technical discussion than an interrogation.

3

HR Round

30 minutes

Questions Asked:

  • Tell me about yourself and your journey
  • Why do you want to work at Google?
  • Describe a challenging project you worked on
  • How do you handle feedback and criticism?
  • Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
  • Questions about Google's values and culture

My Experience:

This was more of a conversation than a formal interview. The HR was very friendly and genuinely interested in understanding my background and motivations. I shared specific examples from my projects and internships. We also discussed Google's work culture and growth opportunities.

I made sure to ask thoughtful questions about team dynamics, learning opportunities, and the specific role I was applying for. The HR seemed impressed with my research about Google's recent projects and initiatives.

Preparation Strategy

Resources Used:

  • LeetCode: Solved 300+ problems focusing on medium and hard difficulty
  • System Design: Grokking the System Design Interview, System Design Primer
  • Books: Cracking the Coding Interview, Elements of Programming Interviews
  • Mock Interviews: Practiced with friends and used Pramp
  • Google-specific: Read Google's engineering blog, studied their papers

Timeline:

I prepared for about 4 months consistently. First 2 months focused on strengthening DSA fundamentals, next month on system design, and the final month on mock interviews and company-specific preparation.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Trees and Graphs (BFS, DFS, shortest paths)
  • Dynamic Programming (1D and 2D problems)
  • System Design fundamentals
  • Communication skills and problem-solving approach

Advice for Future Candidates

🎯 Focus on Problem-Solving Process

Don't just jump to coding. Clarify requirements, discuss approach, consider edge cases, and then code. Explain your thought process throughout.

📚 Quality over Quantity

Better to solve 200 problems deeply understanding each approach than rushing through 500 problems. Focus on patterns and fundamentals.

🗣️ Communication is Key

Practice explaining your solutions clearly. Use proper technical terminology and make sure the interviewer follows your logic.

🎪 Mock Interviews

Cannot stress this enough - practice with real people. It helps with nerves and communication under pressure.

🏢 Company Research

Understand Google's products, culture, and recent developments. Show genuine interest in the company and role.
